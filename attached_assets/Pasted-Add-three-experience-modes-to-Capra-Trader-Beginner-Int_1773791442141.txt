Add three experience modes to Capra Trader: Beginner, Intermediate, and Pro. Here's exactly how each should work:
On first visit, show a full-screen welcome modal with the Capra Trader logo and three large clickable cards. Each card has a title, a one-sentence description, and an icon or emoji. The three options are:

🌱 Beginner — 'I've never bought a stock in my life'
📈 Intermediate — 'I know what stocks are but I'm still learning the details'
💼 Pro — 'I know my way around charts, ratios, and indicators'

Store the choice in localStorage as capra_mode. Add a small pill in the header showing the current mode (e.g. '🌱 Beginner') that the user can click to switch modes at any time — clicking it reopens the mode selector modal.

BEGINNER MODE — assumes the user knows absolutely nothing about stocks, investing, or finance:

Before the search bar, show this intro text: 'A stock is a tiny piece of ownership in a company. When the company does well, your piece becomes worth more. Type a company name below to get started.'
Replace the word 'ticker' everywhere with 'company name'
The search autocomplete should show full company names prominently with the ticker small and gray underneath
After results load, show a friendly paragraph that reads the results out loud in plain English using the actual numbers — e.g. 'Right now, one share of Apple costs $254.23. If the price drops to $249.15, that is your danger zone — our model suggests selling before it falls that far. We think this stock could reach $264.40 within 6 months, which would be a profit of $10.17 per share if you bought today.'
Every single metric card must have a plain English label AND a one-sentence explanation directly underneath in small gray text — not hidden in a tooltip. For example: 'Safety Net Price — This is the lowest price you should let it reach before selling to protect yourself from bigger losses.'
Remove P/E ratio, market cap, volume, and volatility from the results entirely — too advanced
The Stock Picker intro should say: 'Not sure what to buy? Answer 2 quick questions and we will find some stocks that might work for you based on how long you want to wait and how much risk you are comfortable with.'
After picker results load, show a section called 'How did we pick these?' that explains in simple terms: 'We looked at 40 companies and measured two things: how much their stock price grew recently, and how bumpy the ride was. We found the ones with the best growth and the smoothest ride for your risk level.'
Include a slide-out Glossary panel (accessible from a 'What does this mean?' button) with these terms defined in one plain sentence each: Stock, Share, Portfolio, Risk, Stop Loss, Target Price, Bull Market, Bear Market, Dividend, Index


INTERMEDIATE MODE — assumes the user knows what stocks are but not technical ratios:

Use standard labels like 'Stop Loss' and 'Target Price' but include a short one-line explanation underneath each metric in small gray text
Show current price, stop loss, target price, 52-week high and low, and market cap — but label market cap as 'Company Size' with a note like 'Total value of all shares combined'
Skip P/E ratio and volatility score — too technical
The plain-English walkthrough paragraph is optional and hidden behind a 'Explain these results to me' toggle button
Stock Picker shows the algorithm score and a one-sentence rationale per pick
No glossary button needed but tooltips should appear on hover for any term that might be unfamiliar


PRO MODE — full technical experience, no hand-holding:

Compact layout with all metrics visible at once: current price, stop loss, target price, P/E ratio, market cap, volume, 52-week high/low, daily change
No explanations underneath metrics
Stock Picker shows full algorithm breakdown: avg return, volatility, risk sensitivity score, final score out of 100
No glossary, no tooltips, no plain-English walkthrough
Charts are more detailed


Implementation notes:

The mode should affect EVERY part of the UI — labels, explanations, layout density, and which metrics are shown
Make sure the mode switching is smooth — when a user changes mode the current results should re-render in the new mode without needing to search again
The welcome modal should be beautiful and match the existing Capra Trader color palette: background #f9f4f2, heading color #deb2a9, input color #fbe2d0, primary #dfb38a, font Poppins
The modal should not be dismissable without choosing a mode on first visit
All three modes should feel polished and intentional, not like an afterthought
